FAQS

Generative AI is a sophisticated form of artificial intelligence designed to create original content...

such as text, images, audio, and videos by learning patterns and correlations within large datasets. Unlike traditional AI, which might focus on classification or prediction, generative AI synthesizes new data that has never existed before, appearing creative in its output. The technology works by encoding vast amounts of information into a structured format, often using vector spaces, which represent the relationships within data. When prompted, generative AI decodes this information to produce new, contextually relevant content, ranging from conversational text to photorealistic images. Examples include applications like ChatGPT, which can hold human-like conversations, and DALL-E, which generates images based on textual descriptions. Beyond creative generation, generative AI helps summarize vast amounts of information, automate repetitive tasks, and personalize user experiences by tailoring outputs to individual needs. This makes it valuable across industries such as marketing, healthcare, entertainment, and software development. However, the core of generative AI is its ability to generalize and innovate based on learned data, enabling a wide range of functions beyond simple data retrieval or rule-based automation.
